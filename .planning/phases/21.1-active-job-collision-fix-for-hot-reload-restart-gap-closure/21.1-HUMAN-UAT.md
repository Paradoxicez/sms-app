### Latency observation

Single-PATCH cycle observed at **~16-60s** wall-time, not the original target of "~5s":

- `gracefulRestart` fires immediately on signal (< 100ms)
- FFmpeg child receives SIGTERM and exits within 5s grace
- BullMQ RESTART job retry has exponential backoff; first observed retry took ~16s
- Some PATCHes are recovered via `CameraHealthService` 60s dead-stream tick instead of BullMQ retry

This is acceptable for the phase goal ("PATCH actually takes effect, not silently lost") but documents that the worst-case wall-time can hit ~60s when CameraHealth is the recovery path. **eventually-consistent rather than near-real-time** — fine for hot-reload semantics, but if sub-10s is required, follow-up work would need to tune BullMQ retry backoff or short-circuit CameraHealth on this path.

Phase goal in CONTEXT.md says "within 30s for the common case" — common case (BullMQ retry) ≈ 16s ✓; outlier (CameraHealth recovery) ≈ 60s.
